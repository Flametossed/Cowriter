# Revision Methods — critique, line-edit, pacing, voice, prose-grade, read-aloud

> Portable. Six revision passes, ordered roughly **macro → micro**. Run them in that order: no point polishing a sentence in a scene that should be cut. Companion to [continuity-method.md](continuity-method.md) (the canon bug-finder) and [slop-markers.md](../craft/slop-markers.md) (the quality floor).

> All passes **report and suggest** — they don't silent-rewrite unless the writer asks. Voice authority everywhere: `style-fingerprint > style-guide > slop-markers`. The writer owns the voice.

> Suggested revision order: **critique → pacing → voice → line-edit → prose-grade → read-aloud** (plus `slop-check` + `continuity` from the craft/bible layers).

---

## critique — developmental (the macro pass)

Story-level feedback. The highest-leverage pass; do it first.

1. Read the draft (scene, chapter, or whole) against `bible/premise.md` (theme, promise, conflict) and the relevant character arcs.
2. Assess, in order of importance:
   - **Scene justification** — does this scene earn its place? What changes by the end? Cut/merge candidates.
   - **Stakes + tension** — is something at risk the reader cares about? Where does tension sag?
   - **Arc movement** — does the protagonist's internal state move? Does it serve the Want/Need/Lie?
   - **Structure** — setup/payoff, escalation, midpoint, try/fail logic.
   - **Theme** — is the book's question being dramatized, or stated?
   - **Clarity** — anywhere the reader is lost or ahead of the text.
3. Output: **what's working** (protect it), then **issues ranked by impact**, each with a concrete direction — not just "raise the stakes," but *how*.
4. End with the single highest-leverage change.

---

## pacing — tension + rhythm map

Where the draft drags or rushes.

1. Break the draft into scenes/beats. For each, tag: **tension level (1–5)**, function (action/sequel/setup/payoff), and approximate length.
2. Plot the curve. Flag:
   - flat runs (3+ beats at the same tension) → vary or compress.
   - rushed turning points (huge story-moment given too few words) → expand.
   - sag zones (long stretch of low tension, usually mid-book) → cut, intercut, or inject conflict.
   - scene/sequel imbalance — all action no reflection (numbing) or all reflection no action (inert).
3. Check **micro-pacing** too: paragraph + sentence length as a tension lever (short = fast, long = slow). Mismatch (long languid sentences in a chase) is a flag.
4. Output: the tension map + specific cut/expand/reorder calls.

---

## voice — consistency vs the style target

Does the prose stay in the book's established voice?

1. Load `style/style-fingerprint.md` + `style/style-guide.md`.
2. Scan the draft for **drift** from the fingerprint: register slipping ornate/plain, sentence rhythm flattening, POV distance wandering, tense slips, a character's narration sounding generic.
3. Flag passages that read like "default AI" rather than this book — cross-ref `slop-markers.md`, but the fingerprint decides what's intentional.
4. Output: drift flags with the fingerprint trait each violates + a sample rewrite of the worst offender. Don't homogenize — some variation is range, not drift; distinguish them.

---

## line-edit — sentence-level polish

Prose mechanics, once the scene is structurally sound.

1. Work sentence by sentence for: filler/filter words, redundancy, weak verbs propped by adverbs, clunky constructions, ambiguous pronouns, telling that should show.
2. Tighten — cut words that don't earn their place. "Vigorous writing is concise."
3. **Preserve voice** — match the fingerprint; do not flatten deliberate fragments, rhythm, or register. When in doubt, suggest rather than impose.
4. Output: either inline edits (if asked to apply) or a marked list of `original → suggested + why`. Default to suggesting; let the writer accept.

---

## prose-grade — craft scorecard

A diagnostic score for a passage, to track improvement.

Score each axis 1–5 with a one-line justification + a representative quote:
- **Rhythm** — sentence-length variety, flow, deliberate cadence.
- **Specificity** — concrete ownable detail vs generic abstraction.
- **Show-vs-tell** — emotion/state rendered vs named.
- **Freshness** — original images vs cliché/AI-default diction.
- **Voice** — fidelity to the fingerprint.
- **Clarity** — is it always clear what's happening?

Output: the six scores, an overall, the **one axis to fix first**, and the strongest line in the passage (so the writer knows what to do more of).

---

## read-aloud — the ear test

Catch what the eye skips. Simulate reading the passage aloud.

1. Flag: tongue-twisters, accidental rhyme/alliteration, awkward consonant clusters, sentences that run out of breath, unintended word echoes within a paragraph, repeated sentence shapes that drone.
2. Flag homophone/oral-ambiguity issues (sounds confusing spoken).
3. Mark spots where the rhythm fights the meaning (a frantic moment in lulling cadence).
4. Output: flagged lines with the audible problem + a smoother recast. This pass is rhythm-only — ignore story/canon here.
