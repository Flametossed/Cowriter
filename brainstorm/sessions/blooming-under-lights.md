## Chosen spark + Twist (CONVERGE)
- **Picked:** Spark #1 (the seed).
- **The default (named):** Wish-fulfillment harem romcom — American everyman transfers in, charms three pretty upperclassmen who each crush on him; drama club is set dressing. Comps: stock anime harem / exchange-student romcom.
- **Lever used:** Invert + change-the-cost.
- **Twisted concept (v2):** American freshman fiction-writer comes to Japan to escape a family that disapproved of his writing career — he's unknown, unproven, bet everything on the thing they called worthless. He falls in with a campus entertainment circle: three upperclassmen creators across mediums ("drama" = entertainment broadly, not just stage — streamer / musician / alt-creative). They discover his writing by accident and find this nobody can give their content real interior life. He becomes the writer behind the crew. The pull toward him isn't charm — it's that he *sees* them; to be written by him is to be understood. Same gift is the danger: he mines real life, so every script risks exposing the people who finally believed in him.
- **Killed / rejected defaults:** stage-only theater; bland self-insert who charms by existing; girls who crush for no reason; romance as a collection game; drama club as backdrop.

## Premise (locked)
- **Logline:** A disowned young writer, alone in Japan, has to turn three strangers' hidden lives into the work that proves his gift is worth everything he gave up — but the truer he writes them, the closer he comes to exposing and losing the only people who ever believed in him.
- **Want:** internal (prove he's not the failure his family named) with an external scoreboard (the work landing).
- **Romance:** reverse-harem, surface theme; real eventual endgame (which girl = TBD, decide in Plot/Beats).
- **Texture:** age/seniority inversion — he's the freshman *kouhai*, they're *senpai*, but the junior holds the gift. Power flips the usual hierarchy.

## Plot engine
- **Engine:** Every time he can't say no — to a girl, to a project — he deepens a bond and earns the belonging he's starving for, but over-promises and entangles himself further, until the big shared project drags all three into one room and he can no longer be everything to everyone. Secondary cost (the wound's edge): the realest gift he can give each girl is the truth of who she is — put on her stream/song/scene — so every yes is intimacy AND a small exposure. Being everything to all three means someone is always betrayed by his refusal to choose.
- **Irreversible first move:** His piece wins the school writing competition → the first girl reads it, is moved, seeks him out → he says yes. His can't-say-no turns that one yes into every yes after. He's visible now — can't go back to safe and invisible.
- **The squeeze:** belonging / vindication (every yes earns it) vs. his own capacity + honesty (every yes over-promises, so spreading thin betrays each of them and crushes him). Professional and romantic = same pressure.
- **Flaw / Lie:** can't say no, indecisive, piles pressure on himself. Lie ≈ "if I say no, I lose them / let everyone down." Want = prove the gift / be wanted. Need = learn to choose, to say no, to set a boundary. Climax = he finally chooses (the girl, the project, the boundary) or collapses — NOT a duel or power-of-love save.
- **Tone ref:** White Album 2 — nobody's a villain, MC is complicit through weakness, everyone gets hurt anyway. MC is NOT idealized; his indecisiveness causes real damage.
- **Structure shape:** one girl at a time (sequential arcs), first girl = the one who read his competition piece → all converge on a bigger shared project in the back half (the climax arena).

## Beat sketch
<!-- writer wants to stay surprised — kept loose on purpose -->
- **T1 Inciting / first yes:** first girl seeks him out because his winning piece accidentally nailed something true about HER — rattled, not fawning; asks him to write for her; he can't say no. (Sets write-true = exposure immediately.)
- **T2 Pattern locks + first cost:** it works too well — realness exposes her more than she wanted; success + guilt + closeness at once. Plants the WA2 cost.
- **T3 Second yes:** says yes to second girl to avoid facing the first's hurt — avoidance, not romance; friend names it cowardice. Indecision turns culpable.
- **T4 Midpoint (load-bearing):** the three discover their shared link and POOL him into one big project instead of fighting; he says yes to all. Dream + trap snap shut together.
- **T5 Project crushes him:** writing all three true at once, in front of each other; each choice favors one, wounds others; a real betrayal lands via a line he wrote.
- **T6 All is lost (as success):** project lands huge — public win — but one girl exposed past repair, another walks, friend alienated; the win IS the loss. Pays off T2.
- **T7 Climax / the choice (load-bearing):** he finally SAYS NO — chooses one honestly, wounds the others honestly, instead of betraying all three softly. Choosing costs; no clean harem end. (Endgame girl = open.)
- **T8 Resolution:** can say no now, owns the cost — but damage is permanent; not everyone returns. No longer the failure his family named; vindication tastes different. Bittersweet.
- **Checks:** no betrayal-reveal midpoint, no undone sacrifice, no power-of-love save, romance resolves at a cost. "All-is-lost = success" is the engine signature.
