# Premise — The Lamplighter's Daughter

> The spine of the book. One source of truth for what the story *is*.

## Logline
A lamplighter's daughter who can see the dead in lamp-flame must extinguish every light in the city to stop a spreading plague of ghosts — even though darkness is what lets them in.

## Premise (2–4 sentences)
In gas-lit Verran, the dead linger in flame and only Sable, daughter of the city's chief lamplighter, can see them. When the ghosts begin crossing from flame into the living, the Lamplighters' Guild orders the lights doused — but darkness is exactly what the dead have been waiting for. Sable must choose which lights die and which stay, knowing each choice kills or saves.

## Theme
- **Central question:** Is it crueler to keep a comforting light, or to face the dark honestly?
- **Stated as:** Light hides as much as it shows.
- **How it surfaces:** flame motifs, the cost of comfort, things seen only at the edge of dark.

## Central conflict
- **External:** stop the ghost-plague before the city is overrun.
- **Internal:** Sable clings to her dead mother's flame; letting it go is her arc.
- **Philosophical:** comforting illusion vs. honest grief.

## Genre / tone
- **Genre + subgenre:** gaslamp fantasy, gothic
- **Comparable titles (comps):** *Piranesi* meets *Jonathan Strange*
- **Tone:** elegiac, hushed, dread building under stillness
- **Audience:** adult/upmarket fantasy

## Length target
- **Target words:** 90000  <!-- upmarket gaslamp fantasy, single POV; spare voice → ~1.5k scenes → ~60 scenes -->

## Promise to the reader
A melancholy, atmospheric haunting where the magic system *is* the grief.

## What this book is NOT
Not action-forward. Not a romance. No tidy resurrection ending.
