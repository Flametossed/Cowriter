# Outline — The Lamplighter's Daughter
Structure: 3-act
Theme: Light hides as much as it shows. Sable's want = keep her mother's flame; need = let it go to face her grief; lie = "the light keeps her with me."

## Act I — Setup
### Beat 1 — The faces in the flame
- Purpose: establish Sable's gift + the world's dread; plant mother's lamp as the lie-object.
- What happens: Sable watches the night dousing from her window; a ghost in a guttering lamp does something new — it doesn't just look, it *reaches*.
- Turn: routine grief (−) → first sign the dead are crossing (−−, dread spikes).
- Sets up: ghosts crossing flame→living; the mother's lamp. | Pays off: later choice to douse it.
- POV/location: Sable / her room, Candle Street.

### Beat 2 — The Guild's order
- The Guild votes to douse the city. Old Pell dissents. Sable realizes dousing *frees* ghosts.
- Turn: false safety (+) → the cure is the catastrophe (−).

## Act II — Confrontation
### Beat 3–6 (stubs)
- Sable triages which lamps live; each choice costs someone. Mother's lamp becomes the unavoidable question. Midpoint: a ghost crosses fully and speaks in mother's voice.

## Act III — Resolution
### Beat 7–8 (stubs)
- Sable douses the mother's lamp by choice; the dark she feared is also release. Bittersweet, no resurrection.

> Weakest beat: Act II is stubbed — needs the triage escalation broken into distinct cost-beats before drafting deep. Beat 1 is the test target.
