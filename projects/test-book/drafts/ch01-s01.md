# Ch1 / Scene 1 — The faces in the flame

Scaffold: Goal — Sable wants the night dousing to pass like any other. Conflict — a ghost breaks the rule and reaches. Turn — routine grief → the dead are crossing. Hook — the reaching hand.

---

The fog came up Candle Street the way it always did, slow, patient, feeling along the glass for a way in. I watched from the sill with Mother's lamp at my elbow, brass gone warm under my right hand, the wick trimmed close the way she had liked it.

Below me the lighters made their rounds in reverse, dousing what they had lit at dusk. One pole, one breath of dark, and another lamp gave up its small gold. I had learned the shape of it years ago. You watch the flame, and at the last you see the face the flame had been keeping, and the face turns to you because you are the only one who can be turned to, and then it is gone and you have not waved.

I did not wave. I never wave.

But the ghost in the lamp outside the cooper's door did not go when the dark took it. It leaned. A wave of cold fear washed over me as the thing put its hand to the inside of the glass, the way a child presses a window in winter, and the glass was not there anymore, and the hand was in the street.

I stood so fast the stool went over.

Downstairs my father's cane found the boards. Two taps, a drag. He had heard. He was always listening now, the way the sick listen, and the city slept on around us with no idea at all of what had just learned to reach.
